Modest Proposal to Fix The Wage Gap

Vadym Prokopov 2:31 PM

I consider myself a women's rights activist but I find myself upset when women complain about an issue that doesn't exist in our world. Women are not getting paid less than men. I hear all these lies about how for every $1 that a man makes a woman would earn only 80 cents. People sometimes may reference the idea that women are forced to work for less money since they were just born with uteri. I disagree with these people completely since in no way, shape, or form, would anyone discriminate towards people of the opposite gender since we are all the same on the inside (well nearly). The gender wage gap can't exist because the government has passed laws such as the Equal Pay Act of 1963 aimed at abolishing wage disparity based on sex. Many different people come up with other "reasons" to why this gap "exists": 1. Some people say that even when men enter women-dominated fields they still tend to earn more than men on average, they bring up such examples as male nurses earning 10 percent more than female nurses, and janitors making 30 percent more than maids. I find this to be complete lies. Men are way messier than women are on average, I mean if you walked into my college dorm room I can, without a doubt, tell you, you would be walking out with something dirty stuck to you, whether it be dirty clothing or undone homework. So there is no way that a man would earn more than a woman at a cleaning job. Some people may suggest supporting the idea of wage transparency where other people would be able to see what you are getting paid so that everyone has an open ability to negotiate proper and equal incomes, but I find this idea to be completely idiotic since if our country was to adopt wage transparency then how would I be able to tell people that I'm making nearly 200k every year and make all my colleagues feel jealous of me, so this idea is completely out of the question. Another solution people provide is to encourage women to develop better negotiation skills since only 57 percent of men negotiate their salaries compared to 7 percent of women. I find this fact to be completely false since somehow my mother has always tricked me to do my chores even though I have never wanted to. This is just false since women are more persuasive than men are on the usual. I find these arguments to be completely irrational since I have not seen them play out in my day to day life. So I say we stop worrying about the wage gap and continue on with our lives as usual. I (quite literally) see no reason to change any of our policies towards the wage gap. My final statement is the following:

Don't fix whats not broken.
Background on the author: The author is a, Michael Stevens. He is a 34 year old male living in southern Indiana in his mother's basement.
